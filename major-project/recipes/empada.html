Chicken Empada

Time: 1h 15 min

Servings: 12-15

Ingredients – Dough

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 5 tablespoons cold butter
  • Cold water (10 to 15 tablespoons, just enough to bind the dough)
  • 1 egg yolk for brushing

Ingredients – Filling

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cooked and shredded chicken breast
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Half a packet of tomato sauce
  • 3 tablespoons chopped parsley

Instructions

  1. Melt the butter in a pan and sauté the onion until golden. Add the garlic and cook briefly.
  2. Add the shredded chicken, season with salt, pepper, and paprika, and mix well.
  3. Stir in the tomato sauce and parsley. Mix and set aside to cool.
  4. In a bowl, combine flour, salt, and cold butter. Mix with your fingertips until crumbly.
  5. Add cold water gradually until a smooth dough forms and stops sticking to your hands.
  6. Take small portions of dough and press into empada molds, covering the bottom and sides.
  7. Poke small holes in the dough and add the chicken filling.
  8. Use another piece of dough to cover the top, trimming the edges.
  9. Brush with the beaten egg yolk.
  10. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 40–45 minutes, until lightly golden.
