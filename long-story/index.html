How many World Cups have Brazil won?

Scroll Down

1958 World Cup: Brazil victorious for the first time

The tournament in Sweden will forever be remembered for the rise of a young Pelé, who changed football history.

1962 World Cup: Back-to-back triumphs, even without a hero

Fans were eager to see Pelé shine again in Chile, but after an early injury in the group stage, the 21-year-old had to sit out the rest of the tournament.

1970 World Cup: Pele’s redemption

12 years after their first triumph, many thought Brazil’s golden era and Pelé’s brilliance had come to an end. But with one of the greatest teams ever assembled, featuring Pelé, Jairzinho, and Carlos Alberto, Brazil claimed the Jules Rimet Trophy for the third time with a 4–1 win over Italy.

1994 World Cup: Brazil light up the USA

The 1994 World Cup wasn’t Brazil’s most spectacular run, but it marked their return to glory after 24 long years without lifting the trophy.

2002 World Cup: Brazil become five-time champions in Japan and Republic of Korea

The 2002 World Cup brought another legendary Brazilian squad, featuring a fearsome attack with Ronaldo, Rivaldo, Ronaldinho, and Kaká, alongside stars like captain Cafu and Roberto Carlos.
